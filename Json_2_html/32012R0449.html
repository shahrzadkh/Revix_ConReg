    <div class="crrArticle"> <p>This Regulation lays down the rules which determine the information to be provided to ESMA by a credit rating agency in its application for:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>registration, as set out in Annex II to Regulation (EC) No 1060/2009; or</li> <li>certification and for the assessment of its systemic importance to the financial stability or integrity of financial markets referred to in Article 5 of Regulation (EC) No 1060/2009.</li> </ol> </div>Subject matterArticle 1

    CONTENTSUBTITLETITLE
    <ol class="crrNumList"> <li>An application for registration shall be provided in an instrument which stores information in a way accessible for future reference and which allows the unchanged reproduction of the information stored.</li> <li>A credit rating agency shall give a unique reference number to each document it submits. It shall ensure that the information it submits clearly identifies to which specific requirement of this Regulation it refers and in which document that information is provided. The credit rating agency shall submit the table set out in Annex I as part of its application to clearly identify the document in which information required under this Regulation is provided.</li> <li>If a requirement of this Regulation does not apply to a credit rating agency’s application, it shall state this in the table set out in Annex I and provide an explanation.</li> <li>Where a group of credit rating agencies applies for registration, the application shall clearly identify each credit rating agency to which the information applies. When the same information applies to more than one credit rating agency within the group of credit rating agencies, for the purpose of filling in the table of Annex I, the same reference number shall be given for the common information.</li> </ol>Format of the applicationArticle 2
    <div class="crrArticle">Any information submitted to ESMA during the registration or certification process shall be accompanied by a letter signed by a member of the credit rating agency’s senior management or a representative authorised by the senior management, attesting that the submitted information is accurate and complete to the best of their knowledge, as of the date of that submission.</div>Attestation of the accuracy and completeness of the applicationArticle 3
    <div class="crrArticle">Any information regarding the number of employees shall be provided on a full time equivalent basis calculated as the total hours worked divided by the maximum number of hours subject to compensation within a working year as defined by the relevant national law.</div>Number of employeesArticle 4
    <div class="crrArticle"> <p>Any information regarding the class of credit ratings shall use the following ratings classes:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>sovereign and public finance ratings;</li> <li>structured finance ratings;</li> <li>corporate ratings:</li> <ol class="crrRomanList"> <li>financial institution including credit institutions and investment firms;</li> <li>insurance undertaking;</li> <li>corporate issuer that is not considered a financial institution or an insurance undertaking.</li> </ol> </ol> </div>Class of credit ratingsArticle 5
    <ol class="crrNumList"> <li> <p>Policies and procedures provided in an application shall contain or be accompanied by:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>an indication of who is responsible for the approval and maintenance of the policies and procedures;</li> <li>a description of how compliance with the policies and procedures will be enforced and monitored and who is responsible for this;</li> <li>a description of the measures undertaken in the event of a breach of the policies;</li> <li>an indication of the procedure for reporting to ESMA a material breach of the policy or procedure which may result in a breach of the conditions for initial registration or certification.</li> </ol> </li> <li>A credit rating agency may fulfil the obligation to provide information regarding its policies and procedures under this Regulation by submitting a copy of the relevant policies and procedures.</li> </ol>Policies and proceduresArticle 6
    <div class="crrArticle"> <p>A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>the information listed in Annex II to this Regulation;</li> <li>an excerpt from the relevant commercial or court register, or other form of evidence of the place of incorporation and scope of business activity of the credit rating agency, as of the application date.</li> </ol> </div>Identification, legal status and class of credit ratingsArticle 7

    <ol class="crrNumList"> <li> <p>A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>a list of each person who directly or indirectly holds 5 % or more of the credit rating agency’s capital or of voting rights or whose holding makes it possible to exercise a significant influence over the management of the credit rating agency;</li> <li>the information set out in points 1 and 2 of Annex III in relation to each such person.</li> </ol> </li> <li> <p>A credit rating agency shall also provide the following information to ESMA:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>a list of any undertakings in which a person referred to in paragraph 1 holds 5 % or more of the capital or voting rights or over whose management that person exercises a significant influence;</li> <li>an identification of their business activity referred to in point 3 of Annex III.</li> </ol> </li> <li> <p>Where a credit rating agency has a parent undertaking, it shall:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>identify the country where the parent undertaking is established;</li> <li>indicate whether the parent undertaking is authorised or registered and subject to supervision.</li> </ol> </li> </ol>Owners and parent undertaking of a credit rating agencyArticle 8
    <div class="crrArticle">A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with a chart showing the ownership links between any parent undertaking, subsidiaries and any other associated entities established in the Union and their branches. The undertakings shown in the chart shall be identified by their full name, legal status and address of the registered office and head office.</div>Ownership chartArticle 9

    <div class="crrArticle">A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with an organisational chart detailing its organisational structure, including a clear identification of significant roles and the identity of the person responsible for each significant role. Significant roles shall include at least senior management, persons who direct the activities of the branches and senior rating analysts. Where the credit rating agency conducts ancillary services, the organisational chart shall also detail its organisational structure in respect of those services.</div>Organisational chartArticle 10
    <ol class="crrNumList"> <li>A credit rating agency shall provide to ESMA information regarding its policies and procedures in relation to its compliance function as set out in point 5 of Section A of Annex I to Regulation (EC) No 1060/2009, review function as set out in point 9 of Section A of Annex I to Regulation (EC) No 1060/2009 and information regarding its policies and procedures established to meet the requirements set out in points 4 and 10 of Section A of Annex I to Regulation (EC) No 1060/2009.<br>The information provided under this paragraph shall include the information set out in Annex IV points 1, 3 and 4.</li> <li>Where the policies and procedures referred to in paragraph 1 are carried out at group of undertakings level, a credit rating agency shall also provide ESMA with the information set out in Annex IV point 2.</li> <li>A credit rating agency shall also provide ESMA with the information set out in Annex X.</li> </ol>Organisational structureArticle 11
    <ol class="crrNumList"> <li>A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with information regarding its internal corporate governance policies and the procedures and terms of reference which govern its senior management, including the administrative or supervisory board, its independent members and, where established, committees.</li> <li>Where a credit rating agency adheres to a recognised corporate governance code of conduct, it shall identify the code and provide an explanation for any situations where it deviates from the code.</li> <li>A credit rating agency shall provide the information set out in points 1 and 2 of Annex V on the members of its administrative or supervisory board.</li> <li>A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with a copy of the documents referred to in point 3 of Annex V.</li> </ol>Corporate governanceArticle 12

    <ol class="crrNumList"> <li>A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with a copy of its annual financial reports, including individual and consolidated financial statements where applicable, for the three financial years preceding the date of the submission of its application to the extent available. Where the financial statements of the credit rating agency are subject to statutory audit within the meaning given in Article 2(1) of Directive 2006/43/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 17 May 2006 on statutory audits of annual accounts and consolidated accountsOJ L 157, 9.6.2006, p. 87., the financial reports shall include the audit report on the annual and consolidated financial statement.</li> <li>Where the financial reports referred to in paragraph 1 are not available for the requested period of time, a credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with an interim financial report.</li> <li>Where the credit rating agency is a subsidiary of a group of undertakings, it shall provide the annual financial reports of the parent undertaking for the three financial years preceding the date of the submission of its application.</li> <li>A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with a description of the measures it has adopted to ensure sound accounting procedure.</li> </ol>Financial reportsArticle 13

    <ol class="crrNumList"> <li> <p>A credit rating agency shall provide ESMA with the following information:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>the general information requested in points 1 to 10 of Annex II;</li> <li>the information regarding its owners referred to in Article 8;</li> <li>the organisational chart referred to in Article 10;</li> <li>details on the arrangements in place to prevent, the preceding three years, the number of employees contracted and involved in the rating and ancillary services both permanent and temporary;</li> <li>if the applicant has a branch, the number of employees involved in the rating and ancillary business in each branch;</li> <li>the number of rating analysts contracted to the applicant including, if the credit rating agency has a branch, the services and the timeframe for their commencement;</li> <li>the revenue generated over the past three years by the credit rating agency from rating and ancillary services as a proportion of total revenue, presented on a financial year basis.</li> </ol> </li> <li> <p>A credit rating agency shall also provide ESMA with the following information regarding the credit ratings it issues or proposes to issue:</p> <ol class="crrCharList"> <li>the class of credit ratings;</li> <li>the rating nomenclatures used for each class of credit rating;</li> <li>the definition of any rating action and statuses used by the credit rating agency;</li> <li>details of whether the credit rating agency produces solicited or unsolicited ratings or both;</li> <li>for each class of credit rating, the number of years of experience it has in producing these ratings;</li> <li>for each class of credit rating, the current or expected proportion of public ratings and private ratings.</li> </ol> </li> <li>The
